The "Ratio" Dealbreaker

This is the #1 confusion point on Reddit. The M1 Pro usually has a 1:1 ratio, meaning 50lbs feels like 50lbs. The M2 has a 2:1 ratio, meaning 50lbs feels like 25lbs. Why does M2 do this? For longer cable travel and smoother functional movements.

Why choose 1:1 (M1 Pro)?

Better for heavy, static exercises like Lat Pulldowns or Seated Rows. You don't need to stack as much weight to feel resistance.

2

Why choose 2:1 (M2)?

Crucial for "Functional" moves (Cable Flyes, Woodchoppers). The cable extends further (usually 80"+ vs 60") so you don't run out of line, and the resistance is smoother.

The "M2 3D" Confusion

You mentioned "M2 3D". In Smith Machine terminology, "3D" usually refers to a bar that moves horizontally and vertically (like a Jones machine).

Note: The RitFit M2 is generally a Linear Smith Machine (vertical only). Users often search for "3D" because of the "3-in-1" marketing (Rack + Smith + Cables) or confuse it with competitor 3D models. Don't expect horizontal bar movement on the standard M2.
